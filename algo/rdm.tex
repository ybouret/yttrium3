\subsection{Increase of Broken Laws}
Let $\vec \alpha_i$ be the representation of $L$ broken laws:
\begin{equation}
	\forall i, \langle \vec \alpha_i \vert \vec A \rangle = - x_i < 0
\end{equation}
We want to find the minimal $d \vec A$ such that:
\begin{equation}
	\forall i, \langle \vec \alpha_i \vert \vec A + d\vec A \rangle = 0 \implies \langle \vec \alpha_i \vert d \vec A \rangle = x_i > 0 
\end{equation}

We form:
\begin{equation}
	\bm{\alpha} =
	\begin{bmatrix}
	\vec \alpha_1\\
	\vdots\\
	\vec \alpha_L\\
	\end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}

We want to minimize using $\vec \mu \in \mathbb{R}^L$ Lagrange multipliers:
\begin{equation}
	\mathcal L = \dfrac{1}{2} \delta \vec A - \langle \vec \mu \vert \bm\alpha\;\delta \vec A - \vec x \rangle
\end{equation}

We obtain:
\begin{equation}
	\delta \vec A = \bm\alpha^T \; \vec \mu \implies (\bm\alpha\bm\alpha^T) \vec \mu = \vec x
\end{equation}

The increase in concentrations is:
\begin{equation}
	d \vec A^2 = \dfrac{x_s^2}{\vec \alpha^2} = \left( \dfrac{x_s}{\vert\vec\alpha\vert}\right) ^2
\end{equation}
The new concentrations are:
\begin{equation}
	\vec A ' = \vec A + d \vec A = \vec{A} - \dfrac{1}{\vec \alpha^2} \langle \vec \alpha \vert \vec A \rangle \vec \alpha 
	= \dfrac{1}{\vec \alpha^2} \left[ \vec \alpha^2 \bm I - \vert \vec \alpha \rangle \langle \vec \alpha \vert \right] \vec A
\end{equation}
